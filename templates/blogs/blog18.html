How to find a reliable Electrician

  • Tom Cakebread
  • 13 September 2021
  • Tags

Electricians are trained and experts in electrical engineering, so you don't have to be.

Can you imagine doing your own home electrical repairs? Even with many hours of research and long tutorials, the risks are not worth taking. You might get electrocuted if you mistakenly touch live wires, or you might start an electrical fire. Worse still, you might put your whole property and family at risk of an electrical related incident or accident. The risks are just not worth taking.

Changing your light bulbs and lifting a tripped circuit breaker should be the furthest you should go. Otherwise, if you experience electrical problems, you need to find a reliable electrician.

This guide will walk you through the main considerations and activities to undertake, when you are looking to find and hire a reliable electrician.

When do I need an electrician?

Yes, while it might be tempting to save money by doing your electrical repairs yourself, it is better to have a qualified electrician do the job for you and for the the following important reasons:

Troubleshooting

Sometimes, a single electrical issue might indicate a bigger underlying or dependent problem. A qualified electrician can troubleshoot your electrical system, identifying more electrical issues and repairing them. It is also somehting a qualified electrician can do. However, if you decide to correct the electrical problem yourself, you might end up not finding the root cause of the issue as well as creating a more dangerous electrical setup. You may also not find and deternine the exact cause itself and this can be frustrating. All thses are reasons support getting a qualified and experienced electrician to undertake the job or project.

To avoid costly repairs

If you want to avoid expensive repair costs then we would strongly recommend you get a qualified electrcian. You might know how to change a burnt-out light buld, or lift a tripped circuit breaker. But the testing, repairing, fixing, wiring and securing of electrical circuits and panels, is far more complicated and should not be undertaken without the correct knowledge, training, qualification and experience.

If you insist on doing it, you might destroy your electrical system, and this will be very costing to you, to get the repairs for the damage you casued and the original fault you tried to fix. If you hire an electrician when you have electrical problems, the job will be done right and in a dafe way.

To ensure your family’s safety

You wouldn't drive down the motorway at 70 miles per hour, with your family, with brakes that you fitted and not a nechanic.

So whoy would you take the same risk by doing electrical wiring or re-wiring yourself. Especially if you are introducing your family or loved ones to live in the same unsafe setup.

Deciding to do a DIY project for your electrical issues is a common oversight in that people opt to do the work themselves, as they want to save money, or think it is easy, but this is extremely dangerous and can put you, your family and others at risk. If you have not got the essential skills, you are not qualified, you are not aware of the safety precautions to take. It is very likely that you can cause an electrical related incident such as a fire or expose the live wires, which can cause someone to get an electric shock if they come into contact with the live wires.

However, you can protect your family and others, by hiring a professional electrician trained in safety procedures, technical skills, and is guranteed to use the right equipment, resources and correct protective gear, to solve the electrical project or issue.

Complying with any building regualation, covenants and codes

Hiring an electrician fulfills the building codes that govern who meets the criteria to do certian work and that work be self approved and valid, in case of any issues. Only trained and licenced electricians should do electrical repairs. If you decide to do your electrical repairs, you might receive bills for damage to your or other properties, you might invalidate any insurances and warranties, or be in violation of regulation concerned with development, alteration or expansion. All this to save a bit of money, would not be worth the risk.

Most common electrical problems at home

Are you experiencing electrical problems in your home? Do you know how to find an electrician? If left unsolved, electrical problems might damage your electrical appliances and wiring or cause an electrical fire. Therefore, to avoid safety hazards, call your electrician once you notice an electrical problem.

Read on and learn about the most common electrical problem you are likely to experience, this is a list of the common problems, so is not exhaustive.

Recurring electrical surges

An electrical surge happens when there is an oversupply of power in the power line to your home. A power surge can destroy or damage your powerline, wiring and even appliances. Lightning strikes and errors from the power companies can cause recurrent electrical surges.

Power surges or 'transients' as they are known, can be described as a brief overload of voltage in a circuit, or wiring infrastructrue or loom. They are registered on the power wave graph, as a distubance, and will usually have a temporary, but abnormally high amplitude when compared to the normal shape of a wave modulation graph at a given intercept on the wave graph.

These 'surges' can damage, errode, or even destroy electronic equipment in the home. It is important to note that 'transients' can reach amplitudes of thousands of volts, which is extremely dangerous, although they tend to last only micro-seconds in terms of unit of measure of time. Transients freqeucy of occurrences are usually very low, but this is due, to the destructive damage they causes after their first occurence. They prevent the system or circuit from supporting the conduction required to preserve the continutiry of electrical power flow.

We know a bit about surges but how do they occur

Equipment in the design of a circuit is recommended to operate a (nominal) voltage, i.e. A voltage that does not naturally cause a surge. Nominal voltages typically are based on an alteranting current or directional reverse of current, at regualar intervals for designated power supplies.

We can see that any change outside the nominal in power will cause an electrical power surge. This change happens, when for example, we switch an appliance on or off. The surge is the overwhelming distribution of elecrtical energy, that exceeds the needs of the nominal requirment. There are different contributors to surges and we will cover this below:

Internal

This is the normal operation that is undertaken when connecting to the electrical grid. The surge is not always apparent based on the fail safes in the curcuit i.e. fuses and circuit breakers are the main devices.

Switching on and off a system demands uncertain electrical loads from the grid system this uncertainty is managed by the systems inbetween. Collective systems or mistakes in circuit design, and/or bad luck, can all result in an overlaod or short circuti and other problems which can damage the circuit and the appliance.

factors that cause surges

Load management

All electrcial systems are part of a design schema. These schemas are subject to inherent surge risk and there are a number of these to consider, we have presented some below:

1. Ground / earth connecting faults

2. Rapid switching on and off of switches

3. Fault clearing and failure to resolve secondary issues in the circuit

4. Power system switch or backup recovery from an outage

5. Fault during arc initiation, gradual attenuation failure by not managing the initiation programme correctly

6. Loose connections

7. Dischrage of devices that are inductive, to offload surplass energy

Magnets and inductive coupling

Electricity flow in a system creates a magnetic field, see Ampere's theory on magnetic field generation. When an elcetric field is formed, the volume of space and change in energy, causes atoms to behave in a certain way, although this is out of scope for this blog, it does bear considering.

Appliances like HVAC and fridges operate on these principles

Static electricity

An electrostatic dischage, operates over a range of conditions, recurrences, and hertz ranges. Typically the volatage on the human body can vary widely but most of us have experienced static shock from firction driven activity, coupled with the right conductive material.

External sources

This is more difficult as there are more factors. Lightining can cause the biggest of issues, but some other factors include damage through reperation, expansion, or development of the infratrusture. Other consideration are more localised lightining strikes, vandalisim and poor equipment setup. It is important to know that this is a cost weighted factor, a lighting strike cam flow into the homem and cause an overload to appliances and or devices from local or further out sources.

If you notice frequent power surges, look through your home for connected electronic devices and disconnect them from your home grid. You can also use a power surge protector to detect and protect your appliances and electrical devices.

If you still experience a frequent power surge, call a certified electrician to inspect, identify and correct the problem.

Frequent power sags and deeps

Power sags and deeps occur when the power voltage to your home grid is reduced slightly, this can cause your light to dim for a short time. It happens when you use substandard or faulty electronic devices. Also, sags and deeps occur because of faulty and old wiring in the property, or when multiple appliances consume a lot of electricity this exerts a load on the system, at the same time and causes the sag to occur as it cannot support a continous demand for power by the systme.

Causes of power sags

Here are a few things that can cause sags, this list is not exhaustive:

-If the grounding line is faulty, this will cause a voltage sag until the fuse or the circuit breaker used in the system is repaired

-Spontaneous changes in the load in the grid or in the circuit

-In homes they occur when heavy duty appliances like fridges, air-conditioner etc start up

Power sags and deeps might affect your heating and cooling system. The drop in power voltage forces the system to work harder to maintain the ideal temperature in your home, this raises your electricity requirement and cost, resulting in higher bills. To prevent power sags and deeps, try unplugging appliances you are not using. If this does not work, hire an electrician to investigate the cause of the sag and to fix the problem.

Circuit breakers tripping often

Tripping happens if too many devices with high wattage, draw electricity from the same socket. The circuit breaker interrupts the power flow to prevent damage. An overloaded power board or loose wiring can also cause tripping.

If your circuit breakers keep tripping, you can lose power to some areas in your home. This type of regular tripping of the breaker, could also indicate a more complex electrical problem.

You can prevent tripping by using low settings on devices connected to the electricity. You can also reduce the electricity used in a single circuit, especially while using high voltage devices, like blow dryers and microwaves. If the circuit breakers still trip, you need to find and hire a qualified electrician.

Extremely dim or bright lights

Have you noticed that the lights in your home are too bright or too dim? It mostly happens when the power voltage fluctuates and the resulting higher voltage brightens the, reverse is also the same the lights while lower voltage dims them.

Your light brightness might fluctuate if you use light bulbs with different voltages you should ensure you use the right voltage or wattage light for the room. Other problems that can cause irregular light dimming or brightening, are problems with the electrical grid, circuit overloads, faulty wiring and bad or open neutral connections.

Extremely bright or dim lights can cause arcing (an electrcal current jumps a gap in the circuit) this is very dangerous as it can short circuit and damage the system and wiring, and/or blow the fuse. Worse (but equally likely) is a spark can start a fire in your home. Therefore, it is important to address the cause of the problem in the right way urgently.

If you notice that your lights fluctuate between very bright and dim, you should ensure the bulbs in your home have similar wattage and the holders are screwed correctly. If nothing changes find a reliable electrician to fix the problem.

Light bulbs which burn out quickly

Are your light bulbs burning out quickly? A newly replaced bulb of good quality should serve you 900 hours for normal light and an average (although it varies) of 50,000 hours for an LED light. Lights burn out faster than normal due to bad circuitry, poor wiring and high voltage. If you buy a faulty batch of lights this could also cause a similar quick burnout problem.

If your lights are burning out quickly, your home might be experiencing a power surge which can cause damage to your lightbulbs, electrical components and appliances.

Trying another brand of light bulbs, might help to reduce quick burnout. However, if the problem persists, hire a licenced electrician to check the electrical supply in your home and fix the problem.

Light switches are not working correctly

Have you tried switching the lights, but they are not turning on? You might have circuit or wiring problems. Substandard material or poor installation might also affect the usefulness of your switches.

If your switches are not working correctly because of loose wiring, switching them on will not help, this could just increase the time to failiure.

To fix the problem, ensure that your circuit breaker hasn't tripped. You can also fix your bulb in another light fixture to ensure it's working. If the switch still doesn’t work, call an electrician.

Overloaded circuit

Have you ever plugged a device into a socket, and it tripped your circuit breaker? If yes, your circuit could be overloaded or you need to inspect for faulty wiring. An overload happens when a device or an appliance uses more electricity than the circuit can safely handle. It can occur when using many devices or appliances in a single circuit. An overload also happens when you have a faulty fuse or few circuits controlling many devices in your home.

It is important to fix overloaded circuits immediately. If electricity passing through your circuit is more than the circuit can handle, the temperature of the conductive wire will increase as it heats up. The higher temperatures of the conductive material, will exceed the melting point of the insulating jacket or compund, thus melting the insulation. The wires will be exposed and this might cause a spark, leading to an electrical fire.

You can reduce circuit overload by ensuring that devices connected to the circuit use electricity limiter or are consistent with what your circuit is designed to handle. You can also disconnect devices that are not in use. If your circuits are still overload, hire a qualified electrician to inspect your electrical connection and fix the problem.

Frequent electrical shocks

Electrical shocks happen when an electrical current travels through your body. They occur as a mild shock when you plug in or turn on a device. Shocks happen due to faulty wiring or faulty appliances. If you live in an older home, poor insulation might be causing these shocks.

It would help if you found a solution to a potential electrical shock situation, because it is dangerous and can cause severe burns, brain injuries, loss of limbs and even death.

To avoid electrical shocks, unplug all devices when they are not in use and call a certified electrician to fix the problem for you.

High electricity bills

It is unusual to get electricity bills higher than your average bills when you don’t have a new electronic device and haven’t changed how you use your electricity. If your bills increase, one of your appliances might be using a lot of energy, or you have faulty circuits and wiring, it could be energy provider has increased prices, or there is money owing so check that also.

High electricity bills might be the result of damaged circuits and wiring, which are consuming more electricity than standard, but they are also dangerous. Fixing them not only reduces your electricity bills, but also ensures you have a safe setup.

You can try reducing your electricity bills by unplugging all devices that are not in use. However, hiring an electrician helps identify the cause of the power surge and and they can also provide you with options and costs for repair.

Flickering lights

Your lights flicker because of quick changes in their brightness. It happens if the light bulbs or the wiring are loose or when there is a current overload.

When the lights in your home flicker, you have a problem within your electrical systems which requires urgent inspection. Getting these inspected and fixed will help you to avoid potentially being electrocution or causing an electrical fires.

Before calling your electrician, you can check and ensure that the bulbs, plugs are fixed and fitted correctly (i.e. not fully screwed in or plugged in). However, you should contact an electrician to identify and fix the problem if flickering continues after you have verified everything.

The average cost of hiring an electrician

If you want to hire an electrical technician to fix your electrical problems, you can either pay an hourly rate, a day rate, or a fixed fee for a specific job. The average hourly rate for an electrician is £40. The rate can reduce or increase depending on your location. If you decide to pay your electrician per day, the average cost is £320. But, depending on your contractor’s rate and your location, the price may be lower or higher.

If you are paying per specific job, the price depends on whether the job is complex or simple. For a simple job like repairing a light switch, the average cost is £85. However, if you require more intensive electrical repairs like rewiring your whole house, it costs more. The average price for rewiring a one-bedroom home is £1900, while you can spend £6500 on a five-bedroomed house.

The process of hiring an electrician

Hiring an electrician is about getting the best services at affordable prices. If your electrical issue is not an emergency, you need to take your time researching to find the best electrician. Follow the steps below while looking to hire an electrician, for the best outcomes:

• Identify four to five electrical contractors so that you can have options.

• Go through their website, read reviews, and learn about their services. You can also ask for referrals or testimonials also.

• Contact the contractor, explain your electrical problems, and ask for their quotes.

• Compare quotes from the different contractors and choose two to three.

• Make sure that the contractor has insurance and a licence.

• Sign a contract with your preferred first choice electrical contractor.

Questions to ask a technician before hiring them

Whether you are hiring an electrician for a simple fixture issue or a more complex wiring issue, you should ask the following questions to ensure you have the best person for the job.

• Can you show me proof that you are insured and licenced?

• How long have you been an electrician?

• Can you share some of your references?

• Do my repairs require any regulatory submission and approvals?

• Can you provide a detailed quote for the repairs

• Is there warranty on parts and work done

How to deal with quotes and estimates from electricians

The quotes you receive from the electrical contractors have the estimated cost of your repairs. The number of quotes you request depends on the type of service you need. If you need to rewire your entire house, don't be surprised if the costs are high. Therefore, you should ask for quotes from five contractors to compare with, and have better or more choices to benchmark. If you only need minimal electrical repairs, you can request quotes from 2 or 3 companies.

After getting quotes from multiple businesses, you should take your time and go through them carefully. Identifying the contractor who meets your requirements takes time. You should start by comparing the quotes for the repair costs and select those that fit your budget. Be careful of the very low quotes because the services you get, might disappoint you.

You should also select quotes with an itemized price list for the service. Itemized pricing shows all costs on the quote, and you won’t be surprised by hidden expenses. If you find several quotes in line with your requirements, you can do more research to help you decide on the best one.

What to expect from your electrcian?

After hiring your suitable contractor, there are qualities that you expect your electrician to have:

Keep an open line of communication

The electrician should understand your needs and do all activities included in your contract. As they go on with the repairs, they should keep an open line of communication with you.

For example, they should take time to explain other problems they have found in your electrical system and give you a way forward. Additionally, they should also present you with solutions and alternatives for the issues. Open communication allows you to know the progress of the repairs.

Good time management

Your electrician should also have good time management skills. They should complete the repairs in time as indicated in their quote unless there are unavoidable issues that might delay the repairs.

Ensure safety

The electrician should exercise safety at all times. Safety should be a priority. Even if experienced and trained, the electrician should not leave bare live wires lying around exposed. Throughout the repairs, they should protect your family and themselves by following the right safety protocols and letting people know what they need in order to keep them safe and od the work safely.

Have the proper knowledge and the right tools

You have already provided details of the electrical problems to repair. You should expect your electrician to come prepared with the right tools (well maintained work not withstanding) and knowledge. Your contractor should also understand the importance of using the right tools. Also, since technology has led to better tools and equipment, your electrician should have tools that make their work easier, and enables them to work more efficiently.

Provide consistent services

Your electrician should be consistent. The work and services provided throughout your project should have the same quality. Consistency shows the skills and experience of your electrician.

Pays attention to the details

The electrician should be very attentive while working on your electrical system. Any distraction might cause your electrician to forget necessary steps that might cost you money and make the area unsafe. Also, the electrician should be orderly while working to ensure that every detail in your electrical system receives attention.
